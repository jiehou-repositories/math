n-fold Cartesian Product of Field

Definition

\(\textbf{F}^{n}\) is the set of all tuples of length \(n\) of elements of \(\textbf{F}\).
\[ \textbf{F}^{n} = \{(x_{1}, \dots, x_{n})\ |\ x_{1}, \dots, x_{n} \in \textbf{F}\ \text{and}\ n \in \mathbb{Z}^{+}\}. \]
For \(1 \leq i \leq n\) and \(i \in \mathbb{Z}^{+}\), \(x_{i}\) is called the \(i^{\text{th}}\) coordinate of \((x_{1}, \dots, x_{n})\)

Operations

Addition \(+:\ \textbf{F}^{n} \times \textbf{F}^{n} \rightarrow \textbf{F}^{n}\)

\(\forall\ (x_{1}, \dots, x_{n}), (y_{1}, \dots, y_{n}) \in \textbf{F}^{n}\), \[ (x_{1}, \dots, x_{n}) + (y_{1}, \dots, y_{n}) = (x_{1} + y_{1}, \dots, x_{n} + y_{n}). \]

Scalar multiplication \(\cdot:\ \textbf{F} \times \textbf{F}^{n} \rightarrow \textbf{F}^{n}\)

\(\forall\ \lambda \in \textbf{F}\) and \(\forall\ (x_{1}, \dots, x_{n}) \in \textbf{F}^{n}\), \[ \lambda (x_{1}, \dots, x_{n}) = (\lambda x_{1}, \dots, \lambda x_{n}). \]
